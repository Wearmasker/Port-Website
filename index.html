ABOUT ME

Hi, I’m Bao Le — a Sound Designer based in the Netherlands, focused on building sound and music that makes games feel alive.

I got into game audio because I’ve always loved games and music, and I wanted to create worlds of my own — not just the story and mechanics, but the feeling behind every moment. While studying Creative Media and Game Technologies , I’ve been learning game design and sound design hands-on, and also teaching myself coding and music theory along the way. I enjoy iterating fast, asking for feedback, and polishing details until everything clicks.

My work often involves synth design, audio implementation, and creating full soundscapes — using Ableton Live , Serum 2 , FMOD, and Unity. I’ve worked as a sound design intern at Gamelab Oost, and I was the sound designer for Viral Impact, a client project for 's Heeren Loo (supporting people with Mild Intellectual Disability). For that game, I created the music, ambience, and SFX in Ableton, then implemented and scripted the audio using Unity + FMOD.

Outside of tools and plugins, I’m motivated by growth — I like collaborating with people who push my skills, and I’m always looking for the next challenge that makes me better. My goal is to work as a sound designer / composer, crafting audio that players feel as much as they hear.
